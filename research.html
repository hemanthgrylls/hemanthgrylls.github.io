Research

Three threads, one question.

How do you simulate high-speed flows accurately and fast enough to actually learn from them? My work runs from the physics of jet noise, through the numerics that resolve it, to the machine learning that compresses what we find.

AREA 01

Supersonic jet noise

When a non-ideally expanded supersonic jet leaves a nozzle, it undergoes intricate self- and external interactions that radiate sound. I study the split between shock-associated noise (screech and its broadband cousin) and the mixing noise that isn't tied to shocks. Pinning down the generation mechanism is the prerequisite for mitigating it.

AREA 02

Numerics for hyperbolic PDEs

The Navier–Stokes equations need discretization before a computer can touch them. I develop high-order algorithms and the codes that run them: methods that are accurate, robust, and scalable, and that are smart about how they spend compute, delivering both fidelity and speed.

AREA 03

Scientific machine learning

Modern ML reaches problems in fluid dynamics that were intractable not long ago: high-dimensional, non-convex, nonlinear. I use it to sharpen flow predictions and to understand dynamics, including predicting far-field noise from near-field flow.
